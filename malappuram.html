Malappuram


A treasure trove of natural beauty, Malappuram is virtually an emerald suspended from the verdant Nilgiris. An exotic ecological niche, it nestles between the sparkling diamonte sea and the blue green Nilgiri heights.The district is rich in forest cover which extends in area of about 760 square kilometers.Of this wide expanse about half which is 325 square kilometers belongs to the reserved forest category while the remaining 434 square kilometers comes under the vested forests territory. In fact, Malappuram gets its name from the combination of the two words ‘mala’ or hill and ‘puram’ or top signifying that its location is at the top of a hill.
